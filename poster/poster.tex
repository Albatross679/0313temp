  \block{Problem \& Motivation}{
    \textbf{Goal:} Translate natural language questions into executable SQL over a 25-table flight database.

    \vspace{0.3em}
    \textbf{Example:}\\
    \textit{``Show me flights from Boston to Denver''} \\
    $\rightarrow$ \texttt{SELECT flight\_id FROM flight WHERE from\_airport IN (SELECT airport\_code FROM airport WHERE \ldots)}

    \vspace{0.3em}
    We compare three paradigms -- supervised fine-tuning, offline preference optimization (DPO), and online RL (PPO, GRPO, CISPO) -- to understand when post-training improves a strong seq2seq baseline.
  }

  \block{Key Findings}{
    \begin{enumerate}[nosep, leftmargin=*]
      \item \textbf{SFT sets a strong ceiling}: T5-base reaches 86.0\% F1 with restricted vocab + schema augmentation (+13.7pp from schema alone)
      \item \textbf{Scale matters}: T5-base (222M) outperforms T5-small (60M) by +6.4pp F1
      \item \textbf{DPO reward-hacks}: preference margin grows without improving SQL correctness -- restricted vocab leaves little room to steer
      \item \textbf{RL is ineffective at 86\% F1}: reward too sparse (50--64\% dead groups); single-step updates reduce PPO/GRPO/CISPO to REINFORCE
    \end{enumerate}
  }
